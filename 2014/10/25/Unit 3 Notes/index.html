Subjects and Verbs （主语、动词）

  • Subjects（主语） Words that identify the actor in a sentence or tell what/who a sentence is about.
  • Verbs（动词） Words that either describe what action the actor is doing or provide a link to the condition/state of being of the
    subject.
  • Punctuation（标点） Symbols that tell readers when a sentence ends.

Subjects(主语)

All sentences must have an actor, the “who or what” a sentence is about. The subject in a sentence is that actor. The subject/actor is also usually in front of the verb in the sentence. To locate a subject/actor, ask yourself who or what the sentence is about.

Examples: 以下可作为主语使用

Nouns (part of speech)

    Trains are still a popular form of transport.
    Books lined the back wall.

Personal Pronouns（人称代词） (part of speech)

    She left the building at closing time.
    They often gather in the courtyard.

Demonstrative Pronouns（指示代词）

    This is the best place to park.
    These are the figs and pistachios that are on sale.
    That really got to me.
    Those fell off the truck.

Possessive Pronouns（物主代词）

Singular

    Mine is the last scone on the table.
    Yours was left in the car.
    Hers came with the card attached.
    His was a mighty blow.

Plural

    Ours describe ways others might interpret art.
    Yours were the last to arrive.
    Theirs won first place.

Interrogative Pronouns（疑问代词）

    Who borrowed my pencil?
    What will happen tomorrow?
    Which seems to be the most popular?
    Whose is the red blanket?

Indefinite pronouns（不定代词）

Singular indefinite pronouns use singular verbs. Plural indefinite pronouns use plural verbs.

    Indefinite pronouns that end in –one are always singular. These words include anyone, everyone, someone, and one.
    Indefinite pronouns that end in -body are always singular. These words include anybody, somebody, and nobody.
    Indefinite pronouns like both, many, others, and several are always plural.
    Indefinite pronouns like any, more, most, and some can be singular or plural, depending on how they are used.

The “understood” subject

you, a pronoun, is the “understood” subject

    Please, go to the store.
    Bring me a glass of tea.

Gerunds（动名词）

formed by adding –ing to a verb: swimming, driving, learning, jogging, writing

    Swimming provides cardiovascular exercise.
    Driving in Los Angeles can be very dangerous.
    Learning is inspirational.

Infinitives（动词不定式）

the preposition to + a verb: to swim, to drive, to learn, to exercise, to agree

    To swim is always a choice.
    To drive can be quite nerve-wracking.
    To learn is a challenge I enjoy.

Verbs（动词）

A verb shows what the subject of a sentence does. 主要分为三类：Action、Verbs、Linking or State of Being Verbs

Action verbs（行为动词）

which make up the majority of verbs, show/demonstrate anaction.

    The phone rang loudly.
    They dance well together.
    The little girl laughed joyfully.
    The team plays aggressively.

Linking or state of being verbs（连系动词）

do not show an action. These verbs explain the condition someone or something is in.（把事物联系在一起，连接名词、主语或代词，大多与感官有关）

    The teacher is ill.
    I am tall and beautiful.
    The sister became class president.
    The cat seems agitated by the attention.

Helping or auxiliary verbs（助动词）

助动词常常放置在句子的主动词之前，帮助描述主动词，包括如下

    May
    Might
    Must
    Could
    Would
    Should
    Can
    Will
    Shall

不规则动词

基本上这类动词主要靠记忆，没有固定形式、法则（过去式、将来式等等）
主语和动词必须保持一致，即，主语是单数（复数），动词就该是单数（复数）。
主语语态：主动语态，被动语态。

Sequence of Tenses（时态）

Simple Present（一般现在时）: They walk
Present Perfect（现在完成时）: They have walked
Simple Past（一般过去时）: They walked
Past Perfect（过去完成时）: They had walked
Future（将来时）: They will walk
Future Perfect（将来完成时）: They will have walked

    Judy saved thirty dollars. (past)
    Judy will save thirty dollars. (future)
    Judy has saved thirty dollars. (present perfect)
    Judy had saved thirty dollars by the end of last month. (past perfect)
    Judy will have saved thirty dollars by the end of this month. (future perfect)
